The metrics cost story

Why the Datadog metrics bill explodes

Many teams don’t unpack how Datadog prices metrics until the bill reflects real instrumentation. Here’s the pattern we hear — and how to address it head on.

What are “custom metrics”?

Metrics you (or the app) emit beyond built-in host/agent defaults: golden signals per service, queue depth, business or product KPIs, fine-grained tags, endpoints, cache hit rate — the telemetry that makes dashboards and alerts actually answer “what broke?” and “who’s impacted?”. Not optional fluff — it’s how teams get real value.

Out of the box isn’t enough

Default host and packaged integrations are table stakes. You don’t invest in observability to stare at CPU charts — you need your apps and your customer journeys instrumented. That means custom metrics (and often lots of them).

That’s where Datadog gets you

The pricing model is built around metering those custom metrics (commonly per metric × host or service). The moment you instrument properly to get value, spend jumps — and sticker-shock conversations follow.

Bottom line: “Defaults don’t run the business — your metrics do. That’s exactly where the meter spins fastest with Datadog.”

Q"The UX in Elastic isn't as good as Datadog."

AThey do, but they still map OTel through their schema and proprietary agents. You don't own your data in native form. With Elastic, what you send is what's stored — no lock-in on your telemetry format.

ADifferent bet. Elastic is going AI-first for cross-schema UX rather than forcing ECS convergence. OTel gets full first-class UX. Prom gets onboarding + migration tooling. AI-generated views close the gap.
